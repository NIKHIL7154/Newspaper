by PMO India

He also spoke about the ongoing challenges, such as the COVID-19 pandemic and the need for international cooperation to address these challenges.

The Prime Minister also spoke about the importance of unity and diversity in India, emphasizing that the country's strength lies in its diversity and the ability of its people to come together and work towards a common goal. He also spoke about the need for inclusive growth, where everyone in the country benefits from the progress and development.

The Prime Minister also spoke about the importance of the digital revolution in India, highlighting the government's efforts to ensure that every citizen has access to digital services and infrastructure. He also spoke about the need for a skilled and educated workforce to drive the digital revolution and the need for international cooperation in this area.

In conclusion, the Prime Minister reiterated his commitment to the welfare of the people of India and the government's efforts to ensure a better future for all. He also urged the nation to come together and work towards a brighter and more prosperous future.

Indian Government Announces New Education Policy

by THE HINDU

The Indian government has announced a new education policy, the first in 34 years, aimed at making Indian education more holistic, flexible, multidisciplinary, and aligned to the needs of the 21st century and the global best practices. The new policy will replace the 10+2 structure of school curricula with a new 5+3+3+4 design, which corresponds to the age groups 3-8 years (Foundational Stage), 8-11 years (Preparatory), 11-14 years (Middle), and 14-18 years (Secondary).

The new policy also includes several other significant changes, such as the medium of instruction until at least Grade 5, but preferably till Grade 8 and beyond, in mother tongue/local language/regional language; greater focus on critical thinking, creativity, communication, and curiosity; no rigid separation between arts and sciences, between curricular and extra-curricular activities, between vocational and academic education; and increased use of technology in teaching and learning.

The new policy is expected to benefit millions of students in India by providing them with a more comprehensive and meaningful education that prepares them for the challenges and opportunities of the future.

India's First Private Lunar Mission Launched Successfully

by SPACE.COM

India's first private lunar mission, TeamIndus, was launched successfully on board a Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh. The mission, which is a part of the Google Lunar XPRIZE competition, aims to land a rover on the moon's surface and transmit high-definition video, images, and data back to Earth.

The TeamIndus rover, named ECA (Ek Choti Si Asha), weighs around 600 grams and is equipped with a camera, a seismometer, and a laser reflector. The rover is designed to travel up to 500 meters on the lunar surface and perform various scientific experiments.

The successful launch of the TeamIndus mission marks a significant milestone in India's space exploration journey and demonstrates the country's growing capabilities in the field of space technology.

India's First Solar-Powered Train Unveiled in Gujarat

by RAILWAY NEWS

India's first solar-powered train, the DEMU (Diesel Electric Multiple Unit) train, was unveiled in Gujarat by the Indian Railways. The train, which is equipped with 16 solar panels on its roof, can generate up to 7.5 kilowatts of power, which is sufficient to meet the electrical needs of the train, including lighting, fans, and information systems.

The solar-powered train, which is a part of the Indian Railways' plan to reduce its carbon footprint and promote sustainable energy, is expected to save up to 21,000 liters of diesel and reduce carbon emissions by 51,000 kg per year.

The successful trial of the solar-powered train marks a significant step towards the Indian Railways' goal of becoming a net-zero carbon emitter by 2030.

When darkness overspreads my eyes

by JOHANN WOLFGANG VON GOETHE

When, while the lovely valley teems with vapour around me, and the meridian sun strikes the upper surface of the impenetrable foliage of my trees, and but a few stray gleams steal into the inner sanctuary, I throw myself down among the tall grass by the trickling stream;

and, as I lie close to the earth, a thousand unknown plants are noticed by me: when I hear the buzz of the little world among the stalks, and grow familiar with the countless indescribable forms of the insects and flies, then I feel the presence of the Almighty, who formed us in his own image, and the breath of that universal love which bears and sustains us, as it floats around us in an eternity of bliss; and then, my friend, when darkness overspreads my eyes, and heaven and earth seem to dwell in my soul and absorb its power, like the form of a beloved mistress, then I often think with longing, Oh, would I could describe these conceptions, could impress upon paper all that is living so full and warm within me, that it might be the mirror of my soul, as my soul is the mirror of the infinite God!
